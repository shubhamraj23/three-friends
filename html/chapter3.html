Chapter 3

A Friend allows you Distance, but is never far away

One complete year had passed since they were back together. There were still frequent talks, group chats, and conference calls between the three; fights were easily cleared, but one person among the three seemed to be unhappy and that was Varun. His usual habit of over thinking was back again. After every fight, he used to think a lot about the issue which started causing him a lot of pain. He became the kind of person who started thinking that everything that happened was due to his fault. But the good thing was that he used to recover soon; usually by disappearing for a day or two and there coming back normally. Meanwhile, Kavya and Shreya were having their own issues between themselves, which used to close down and open up again suddenly. Things had changed for them; they were still best friends though; they still cared a lot for one-another; but the bond was weaker amongst them. They were fighting and sorting out issues, friendship was still going on well when life played its final big game on them.

It was again a small fight between Varun and Kavya, which had been cleared out with the help of Shreya. Varun, with his usual habit of over thinking, started to think over the issue again and again and he disappeared again. The other two thought that he might have again disappeared for a day or two, as was his usual habit. But this time, it had gone longer. One week had passed and there was no sign of Varun. Kavya and Shreya were trying to call him, but he wasn’t receiving, texting him, but there was no reply from him. Kavya started to sense something wrong in all this; she started feeling as if it was due to their fight that he had disappeared and it was all her fault. Shreya kept on consoling her; it wasn’t due to their fight, he might be busy in some work. Shreya started talking to Kavya daily, so that she wouldn’t feel bad about this. Kavya also felt better every time she spoke to Shreya, but deep down in her heart, somewhere still she sensed that it was all her fault.

Ten days passed since Varun had disappeared. Kavya was at Shreya’s house. After three long years, the time had come when they both finally met each other again. But their joy wasn’t complete yet. They were deeply missing Varun; there were no signs of him yet. Suddenly Shreya’s cellphone rang, and to her delight it was Varun’s call. Kavya asked Shreya to answer it and keep the phone on speaker.

As soon as Shreya lifted the call, Kavya said, “Varun, is everything alright? How are you? And where are you?”

“Hi Kavya, seems that you both are together. Nice to see that after a long time, you both met each other,” said Varun in a very low voice.

“Yes Varun, and we are equally desperate to meet you as well,” said Shreya.

There was a small smile on the faces of Kavya and Shreya, but little did they know that it wouldn’t last long. While Shreya was speaking, Varun was continuously coughing and it was Kavya who noticed that first.

“Varun, are you fine. You are coughing and that too badly. Is everything alright?” asked Kavya.

“You are right, Kavya. I am in the hospital, lying on the bed,” said Varun slowly, again in the same low, monotonous voice.

“What happened to you?” exclaimed both of them simultaneously.

“Brain tumor, and it is cancerous in my case,” replied Varun.

“What?” exclaimed both of them again simultaneously.

“Yes, that day after our fight had been resolved; I decided not to talk for a while, and starting thinking over and over again about everything that had happened. As far as I remember, my head was aching badly, and after that I started feeling dizzy. I wasn’t able to breathe properly as well, and after that I collapsed. The next time when I got back to my senses, I was lying in the hospital with my mother in the front of me, crying. My father came to me and said me that I had a serious brain tumor, and it required operation, which took place the next day,” said Varun.

“So, now after the operation, are you fine?” asked Shreya.

“No, the operation was unsuccessful,” said Varun.

These words started echoing in their minds, especially Kavya; who started crying badly. Shreya was also in tears hearing this, but she had somehow controlled herself.

“I have got barely a few more days to survive now,” Varun added.

They both were speechless. Various emotions were flowing through them. All their past memories were going through their mind. And it was heartbreaking for them to realise that such moments would never occur in their lives again.

“Both of you stop crying and listen to me now,” said Varun.

For some time, Shreya and Kavya stopped crying, thinking maybe this was the last time they were getting a chance to hear his voice.

“I have a wish; my last wish, which you both have to fulfil. I want that after I leave, you both will never again fight with each other, for even the smallest of arguments, and will always stay as best friends. Three friends will be down to two friends, but these two will have to remain friends forever,” said Varun.

Varun was coughing badly. His condition was going on worse minute after minute. Kavya and Shreya were still depressed, various things were going through their minds, but they couldn’t speak anything. With a lot of courage, Shreya finally spoke something.

“I promise,” said Shreya. There was confidence in her voice.

“And what about you Kavya?” asked Varun.

There was again silence. Kavya was still crying, and it seemed as if it would take her a lot of time to recover.

“Kavya, do not cry. I know that you might be cursing yourself now. Look, this disease has got nothing to do with the fight that we had. Please stop crying, please Kavya,” said Varun.

His words had had an effect on Kavya. She stopped crying and in a confident voice she said, “Yes, I too promise.”

“Good and you both please do not cry from now onwards. Just remember that all the moments that we had shared together were moments of fun. So, please just smile and live the remaining of your life happily,” said Varun.

“A friend allows you distance, but is never far away. Varun, we will always remember you,” said Kavya in a low voice.

“If lucky, I will call you both back again. Wish you both all the success in your life. Always stay happy and take care. Goodbye,” said Varun.

“Thank you, Varun and Goodbye,” said Kavya.

“Goodbye Varun, you will be missed a lot,” said Shreya.

Such a moment had come in their lives where one was blessing the other two and the other couldn’t even wish for him. That was indeed the last time they heard his voice. The very next day, a small column in the newspaper informed them of his death. Kavya and Shreya were still depressed; they couldn’t believe what had just happened. And neither had they thought as well that their meeting after such a long time would give them such sad news. But they both made sure that his last dream was fulfilled. They were in continuous contact with each other, never fought with each other, helped each other a lot, and always remembered him as well. On the odd occasion, one of them would cry badly, missing him as hell, but there was always the second one for help.

Years passed after that. Varun’s blessings had come true. They both became one of the most successful people in their respective fields; and had become role-models for people worldwide. They both credited all this to one man, and that was Varun, a tribute to him.

13 years had passed since the day Varun had left. The batch of 2014 was back in the school in an alumni meet. Every single person from there batch had come there, except for one, who was being badly missed. The two friends were enjoying the party, but not fully; something, a big part of their lives was missing. The party was about to end; the time had come for a group photograph, but two people were missing. Kavya and Shreya were sitting on the ground, in a corner, with their heads down, crying slowly, at the same place where Varun sat and cried years ago.
